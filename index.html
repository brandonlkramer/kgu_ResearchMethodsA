Statistics with JASP

An introduction to quantitative methods for applied linguistics and language learning research

JASP Software SLA Research Estimation Approach Effect Sizes & CIs

These pages accompany a statistics textbook written for researchers in applied linguistics and second language acquisition. Each chapter covers one statistical test: the conceptual logic behind it, the assumptions it requires, how to run it in JASP, how to read the output, and how to write up the results.

The book takes an estimation approach — emphasising confidence intervals and effect sizes over dichotomous significance decisions. The two foundation chapters explain why, and what to do instead.

How to use these pages

1
Read the concept page

Each chapter has a theory page explaining when and why to use the test, illustrated with worked examples from published SLA studies.

2
Follow the JASP walkthrough

The paired JASP page gives step-by-step instructions with annotated screenshots showing every click and every output value to record.

3
Use the write-up templates

Every JASP page ends with a model reporting paragraph you can adapt for your own results — following APA and field-specific conventions.

Example datasets

Used throughout
  • Correlation Flege et al. (1999) 240 Korean immigrants. Age of arrival × English pronunciation rating. Used to illustrate Pearson's r = −.86.
  • Correlation DeKeyser (2000) 57 Hungarian immigrants. Age of arrival × GJT score. Used for grouped scatterplots.
  • T-Test Leow & Morgan-Short (2004) 77 learners. Think-aloud vs. non-think-aloud groups. Used for the independent samples t-test.
  • T-Test Kim (2013) 9 Korean L2 learners. Sarcasm test at pretest, posttest, and delayed posttest. Used for the paired t-test and the one-way RM-ANOVA.
  • ANOVA Ellis & Yuan (2004) 42 Chinese learners. Planning time (NP / PTP / OLP) × syntactic variety in writing. Used for the one-way between-subjects ANOVA.
  • Factorial Obarow (2004) 64 young L2 learners. Music × Illustrations × Gender on vocabulary gain scores. Used for the two-way factorial ANOVA.
